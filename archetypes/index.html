Step 1 Study Pattern Profiles

Five patterns. One exam.

Most students who plateau on Step 1 are not lacking knowledge. They are running a loop. These profiles describe the five most common ones.

The five profiles
Profile 01
The Collector

Accumulates resources faster than they can be used. Every new source feels like a possible missing piece. The review sessions become impossible as the collection grows.

Typical ceiling: 215–225

"You have three Anki decks open right now, none of them finished."

Profile 02
The Memorizer

Retains isolated facts cleanly. Anki retention above 85 percent. Can recite mechanisms with precision. Still misses questions about all of them. The knowledge is there. The direction of retrieval is not.

Typical ceiling: 220–228

"Score on content quizzes: high. Score on NBMEs: does not match."

Read the full profile → / Go to tools →
Profile 03
The Perfectionist

Cardiovascular has been reviewed three times. The notes are immaculate. Moving on feels premature. The exam date is eight weeks out and four systems have not been opened. The standard being optimized for is not the standard the exam uses.

Actual exam ceiling: 205–215

"Cannot move to pulmonary because cardiovascular does not feel finished."

Read the full profile → / Go to tools →
Profile 04
The Coaster

Strong shelf exams. Honors on clerkships. The first NBME came back at 207. The questions feel like a different exam from the one that produced those results, because they are. The old habits worked. They work for a different task than this one.

Typical ceiling with old methods: 215–225

"The knowledge is there. The format is not familiar."

Read the full profile → / Go to tools →
Profile 05
The Drowner

Sits down to study and reaches for the phone. Three hours later, retention is unclear. Opens a UWorld block and the spiral begins by question four. The content knowledge is often adequate. The cognitive load is not. The intervention is reduction, not addition, and it works faster than the alternatives.

Typical ceiling: 205–218

"Knows this is happening. Cannot stop it from inside the loop."
