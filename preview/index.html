A 51-year-old woman stops menstruating and develops fatigue, bronze skin, and pain in her 2nd–3rd metacarpophalangeal joints. Fasting glucose is elevated; transferrin saturation is 65%. Which is the most appropriate initial therapy?
A.Deferoxamine infusion
B.Serial therapeutic phlebotomy
C.Oral penicillamine
D.Ursodeoxycholic acid
Diagnosis: Hereditary hemochromatosis. Why correct: Phlebotomy depletes iron stores by forcing erythropoiesis and is first-line; chelators are reserved for secondary overload. Pivot: Menopause unveils iron overload in women.
