# Tidbits — 282 Rapid-Recall Pearls

## AB (25)

- Anti-dsDNA + anti-Smith: SLE. Anti-dsDNA tracks disease activity and renal prognosis. Anti-Smith = most specific for SLE
- Anti-histone: drug-induced lupus (DIL). Drugs: Mom is HIPP → Minocycline, Hydralazine, INH, Procainamide, Penicillamine
- SLE: thrombocytopenia from anti-platelet Abs. If all cell lines ↓ → increased peripheral destruction (not aplastic anemia)
- Anti-Hu/-Yo: small cell lung cancer → cerebellar ataxia (paraneoplastic)
- Anti-Jo1: polymyositis/dermatomyositis. Can be paraneoplastic of ovarian cancer
- Anti-GQ1b: Miller Fisher syndrome (GBS variant) = ophthalmoplegia + ataxia + areflexia
- Anti-centromere: limited systemic sclerosis (CREST). Anti-Scl-70 (topoisomerase I): diffuse systemic sclerosis
- Anti-mitochondrial (AMA): primary biliary cholangitis. Anti-smooth muscle: autoimmune hepatitis
- RF (IgM vs IgG Fc): rheumatoid arthritis. Anti-CCP = more specific than RF
- Anti-SS-A (Ro) + Anti-SS-B (La): Sjogren syndrome. Anti-Ro in Sjogren → neonatal lupus + congenital heart block
- Anti-TSH receptor (TSI): Graves disease (stimulates TSH-R)
- Anti-TPO + anti-thyroglobulin: Hashimoto thyroiditis
- Anti-parietal cell + anti-intrinsic factor: pernicious anemia
- Anti-endomysial + anti-tTG IgA: celiac disease. False negative if concurrent IgA deficiency → use tTG-IgG instead
- Anti-GAD + anti-ZnT8: type 1 DM
- Anti-PLA2R: primary membranous GN
- Anti-ADAMTS13: TTP. Anti-GpIIb/IIIa: ITP. Anti-PF4-heparin: HIT
- Anti-21-hydroxylase: Addison disease
- Anti-β2-microglobulin + anti-cardiolipin + lupus anticoagulant: antiphospholipid syndrome. Recurrent miscarriage. False-positive VDRL in SLE. Paradoxically ↑ aPTT but THROMBOSIS (not bleeding)
- Anti-hemidesmosome: bullous pemphigoid. Linear IF on skin biopsy
- Anti-desmoglein 1/3: pemphigus vulgaris. Net-like IF on skin biopsy
- Anti-AChR (post-synaptic): myasthenia gravis. Anti-VGCC (pre-synaptic): Lambert-Eaton. Thymoma → do CXR/CT in MG
- Anti-myosin cross-reacts with mitral valve (rheumatic fever). S. pyogenes M protein molecular mimicry
- Anti-aquaporin 4: neuromyelitis optica (Devic syndrome)
- IgM vs IgG AIHA: Cold AIHA = IgM (CMV, Mycoplasma → acrocyanosis). Warm AIHA = IgG (drugs, CLL) → + Coombs test

## CANCER (31)

- AFP: yolk sac tumor (endodermal sinus tumor) in children. Mixed germ cell tumors (with hCG). HY testicular/ovarian tumor marker in kids
- ALP: placental ALP sometimes ↑ in seminoma
- CA 15-3: breast cancer. CA 19-9: pancreatic cancer. Calcitonin: medullary thyroid carcinoma. CEA: colon cancer. hCG: choriocarcinoma. LDH: dysgerminoma. PSA: prostate cancer
- Prostate cancer metastases = osteoBLASTIC (lights up bone scan). All other cancers = osteolytic (don't light up)
- Spinal metastases: prostate + breast + lung love vertebrae. Epidural cord compression = HY complication
- Choriocarcinoma → lung + brain metastases. Pulmonary nodules + stroke in gynecologic patient = think choriocarcinoma
- Krukenberg tumor = gastric cancer → ovarian metastasis (hematogenous). Signet ring cells with mucin
- Ovarian cancer → omental metastasis ('omental thickening/caking'). Para-aortic LN → testicular + ovarian cancer drainage
- Paraneoplastic: small cell lung → SIADH + ACTH (Cushing) + LEMS + anti-Hu/-Yo (cerebellar ataxia)
- Paraneoplastic: squamous cell lung → hypercalcemia + hypophosphatemia (PTHrp). RCC → hypercalcemia (PTHrp) + polycythemia (EPO)
- Thymoma → myasthenia gravis + pure RBC aplasia (rare). Ovarian cancer → dermatomyositis. Pancreatic → Trousseau sign (migratory thrombophlebitis)
- Hypertrophic osteoarthropathy (clubbing + periostitis) → lung cancer. Next step = CXR
- Gastric cancer → acanthosis nigricans + Sign of Leser-Trelat (sudden ↑ seborrheic keratoses) + Virchow node (supraclavicular LN)
- Stage 0 = in situ (above basement membrane). Microinvasion = crossing basement membrane. Stage highest = distant metastases
- Grade = mitoses + anaplasia. ↑ nucleoli = ↑ protein synthesis = ↑ mitoses. 1:1 nuclear:cytoplasmic ratio = high grade. ↑ Ki-67 = high grade
- Carcinogens: Aniline dyes → bladder TCC (textile worker + hematuria). Arsenic → Mees lines + gardener. Asbestos → mesothelioma (mesothelial cells). Benzene → leukemia. Vinyl chloride → hepatic angiosarcoma
- Carcinogens: Aflatoxin (Aspergillus on peanuts in China) → HCC. Nitrosamines (cured meats) → gastric cancer. 2-naphthylamine (moth balls) → collecting duct cancer. Radon (background radiation) → lung adenocarcinoma in non-smokers
- Malignancy-associated microbes: EBV → nasopharyngeal + Hodgkin + NHL. H. pylori → MALT lymphoma. HBV/HCV → HCC. HHV-8 → Kaposi sarcoma. HPV 16/18 → SCC (genital/anal). HTLV → mycosis fungoides + T-cell leukemia. Schistosoma hematobium → bladder SCC (African lake swimmer). Clonorchis sinensis → cholangiocarcinoma
- Follicular lymphoma: t(14;18), BCL-2, MC indolent NHL. Waxing/waning painless lateral neck mass in adult
- DLBCL: t(3;14), BCL-6, MC NHL overall. Aggressive rapidly growing mass. R-CHOP treatment
- Burkitt: t(8;14), c-MYC, EBV, fastest growing tumor. Jaw (Africa endemic) or abdomen (US sporadic). Starry sky pattern
- Mantle cell lymphoma: t(11;14), cyclin D1, CD5+, CD23-. Distinguish from CLL (CD5+, CD23+)
- Primary CNS lymphoma: HIV + EBV + periventricular. CD4 <100. Vs Toxoplasma = ring-enhancing; treat Toxo empirically first
- CML: t(9;22) Philadelphia chromosome. Myelocytes + promyelocytes + metamyelocytes on smear. Low LAP. Basophilia hallmark. Imatinib (BCR-ABL TKI). Blast crisis = AML or ALL
- CLL: MC leukemia in adults (Western world). CD5+/CD19+/CD20dim/CD23+. Smudge cells. Warm AIHA. Richter transformation → DLBCL
- ALL: MC leukemia in children. TdT+. t(12;21) in children (best prognosis). t(9;22) in adults (worst). T-cell ALL = mediastinal mass in teen male
- AML: MC acute leukemia in adults. Auer rods = pathognomonic. APL (M3) = t(15;17) + DIC → treat with ATRA. Gum infiltration = M4/M5 (monocytic)
- Multiple myeloma: CRAB (Calcium, Renal failure, Anemia, Bone pain). M-spike on SPEP. Bence-Jones = free light chains in urine. Lytic lesions = bone scan NEGATIVE (no osteoblastic). MGUS → 1%/year progression
- Polycythemia vera: JAK2 mutation. ↑ RBCs always + ↑ WBCs and/or Plt. Pruritis after shower (basophilia). Phlebotomy for hyperviscosity
- Myelofibrosis: JAK2. Teardrop RBCs (dacrocytes) + dry tap on marrow biopsy + massive splenomegaly
- Histochemical stains: Acid-fast = Mycobacterium + Cryptosporidium. Giemsa = Plasmodium + Trypanosoma + Leishmania. India ink/mucicarmine = Cryptococcus. PAS = Whipple disease (PAS+ macrophages in lamina propria). Prussian blue = iron (sideroblastic anemia). Silver = H. pylori + spirochetes + Pneumocystis

## CV (13)

- MI complications: 1-3 days = arrhythmia (VF). 3-5 days = papillary muscle rupture (acute MR) or VSD (free wall rupture). 1-8 weeks = Dressler syndrome (immune pericarditis)
- Posterior MI: ST depression V1-V3 + tall R wave. RV infarct: inferior MI + ST elevation in V4R + hypotension without pulmonary edema → give IV fluids, avoid nitrates
- Troponin: most sensitive/specific for MI. Rises 3-4h, peaks 12-24h, lasts 7-10 days. CK-MB peaks 24h → useful for reinfarction detection
- HFrEF mortality-reducing drugs: ACEi/ARB + β-blocker (carvedilol/metoprolol) + aldosterone antagonist + SGLT2i + ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan). Diuretics = symptom relief only
- HFpEF: SGLT2i now guideline recommended. S4 gallop (stiff ventricle). HTN + obesity + elderly women
- AS triad: Angina + Syncope + HF. Prognosis: angina = 5yr, syncope = 3yr, HF = 2yr. Crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at RUSB → carotids. Pulsus parvus et tardus
- Murmur maneuvers: Valsalva/standing ↓ AS (↓ preload → ↓ gradient). Squatting ↑ AS. HCM: same as AS with Valsalva. MVP click moves EARLIER with Valsalva (LV smaller → leaflets prolapse sooner)
- Endocarditis: S. aureus = MC overall. Strep viridans = MC subacute on native valves. S. bovis = colorectal cancer. IVDU = tricuspid (S. aureus). Osler nodes (painful, embolic/immune). Janeway lesions (non-painful, septic emboli)
- AFib: irregularly irregular. CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women) → anticoagulate. Rate control: β-blocker or diltiazem. Check TSH in new AFib (hyperthyroidism). Holiday heart = alcohol → paroxysmal AFib
- Torsades de Pointes = polymorphic VT from ↑ QT. Drugs: quinidine, sotalol, haloperidol, TCAs, macrolides, methadone. Treat: stop offending drug, IV Mg2+, ↑ K+. LQTS1: swimming trigger. LQTS2: loud noise trigger
- Aortic dissection: Type A (ascending) = surgical emergency. Type B (descending) = medical (labetalol + nitroprusside). Target SBP <120, HR <60. Do NOT give thrombolytics. Cystic medial necrosis in Marfan/HTN
- HTN emergency: reduce MAP by 25% in first hour (not rapidly). IV labetalol, nicardipine, or nitroprusside. AA: CCBs + thiazides preferred (less RAAS-dependent, salt-sensitive)
- Atherosclerosis: LDL oxidized → macrophage phagocytosis → foam cells. Fatty streak → fibrous plaque → complicated plaque. Plaque rupture → thrombus → MI/stroke

## DERM (7)

- Psoriasis: HLA-Cw6. Silvery scales on extensor surfaces. Auspitz sign (pinpoint bleeding). Koebner phenomenon (trauma → psoriasis). Nail pitting. Guttate = Group A Strep trigger (2 wks after pharyngitis). Psoriatic arthritis: DIP involvement + dactylitis
- Pemphigus vulgaris: anti-desmoglein 1/3 (IgG). Flaccid bullae. Oral mucosa FIRST. Nikolsky sign POSITIVE. Net-like IF. Painful. Treat: steroids + rituximab
- Bullous pemphigoid: anti-hemidesmosome (IgG). TENSE bullae. Nikolsky sign NEGATIVE. Linear IF. Elderly. Less oral involvement than pemphigus
- SJS/TEN: CD8+ T-cell mediated. Drug causes: allopurinol, aromatic anticonvulsants (carbamazepine, phenytoin), sulfonamides, lamotrigine. HLA-B*1502 + carbamazepine = ↑ risk in Han Chinese/SE Asians. Stop drug immediately
- Melanoma: ABCDE criteria. Superficial spreading = MC type. Acral lentiginous = MC in dark-skinned patients (palms, soles, nails). BRAF V600E = MC mutation. Breslow thickness = MC prognostic factor
- BCC = MC skin cancer. Pearly papule + telangiectasias on sun-exposed skin. PTCH1/Hedgehog pathway. Gorlin syndrome = multiple BCCs + jaw keratocysts + calcified falx. SCC from actinic keratosis (precursor)
- Atopic dermatitis: filaggrin (FLG) mutation. Th2/IgE-mediated. Atopic march: eczema → allergic rhinitis → asthma. Flexural distribution in older children (antecubital, popliteal). Dupilumab (anti-IL4Rα) for moderate-severe

## ENDO (9)

- DKA: ↑ anion gap metabolic acidosis + ↑ ketones + Kussmaul respirations. K+ is high in serum but total body depleted. Give insulin AFTER K+ >3.3. Never give HCO3 routinely
- HHS: glucose >600 + ↑ osmolality + NO significant ketones. T2DM. Mortality 15-20%. Aggressive IVF then insulin
- Hypothyroidism: ↑ TSH (most sensitive). Lateral eyebrow loss (Queen Anne sign). Delayed DTR relaxation (Woltman sign). Myxedema coma: IV T4 + IV hydrocortisone (exclude adrenal insufficiency first)
- Hyperthyroidism: ↓ TSH (most sensitive). Graves = TRAb (TSI). Thyroid storm: P-T-I-T-U (PTU → Iodine [1hr after PTU] → β-blocker → steroids → Tylenol). NOT aspirin (displaces T4 from TBG). I-131 for definitive Tx
- Cushing: exogenous steroids = MC cause overall. Pituitary adenoma = MC endogenous. Ectopic ACTH = small cell lung (↑↑ ACTH, no suppression with high-dose DST). Hyperpigmentation = ACTH-dependent forms only
- Addison: hyperpigmentation (primary only) + hyponatremia + hyperkalemia + hypoglycemia. Anti-21-hydroxylase Ab. Waterhouse-Friderichsen = bilateral adrenal hemorrhage from meningococcemia. Crisis: IV hydrocortisone + IVF
- Pheochromocytoma: Rule of 10% (10% bilateral, malignant, extra-adrenal, familial, pediatric). Headache + diaphoresis + palpitations triad. α-blockade FIRST (phenoxybenzamine) THEN β-blockade. Dx: 24-hr urine/plasma metanephrines
- MEN1: 3Ps (Pituitary + Parathyroid + Pancreatic). MEN2A: pheo + MTC + hyperparathyroidism (RET gene). MEN2B: pheo + MTC + marfanoid + mucosal neuromas
- SIADH: euvolemic hyponatremia + ↑ urine osmolality + ↓ serum osmolality. Small cell lung cancer, CNS disease, Tx = fluid restriction. Demeclocycline (nephrogenic DI) for chronic SIADH

## GENE (31)

- APC gene: FAP = hundreds of polyps; chromosome 5; AD; 100% cancer risk. FAP + soft tissue/bone = Gardner. FAP + CNS = Turcot
- BCL-2: anti-apoptotic; overexpressed in follicular lymphoma via t(14;18). Waxing/waning painless lateral neck mass in adult
- BCR-ABL: t(9;22) Philadelphia chromosome → oncogenic tyrosine kinase → CML. Smear = 'motley mix/soup' of myeloid cells. Low LAP. Tx = imatinib
- BRAF: proto-oncogene in melanoma; codes for serine-threonine kinase
- BRCA1/2: tumor suppressors → breast/ovarian cancer. Function = recombinational dsDNA repair. Prophylaxis = bilateral mastectomy + oophorectomy
- c-KIT: proto-oncogene in GIST (gastrointestinal stromal tumors)
- c-MYC: t(8;14) → Burkitt lymphoma. Codes for transcription factor. Jaw/abdomen mass. Starry sky histology = tingible body macrophages (apoptosis)
- EGFR: adenocarcinoma of lung + glioblastoma multiforme. Erlotinib targets EGFR tyrosine kinase
- HER2/neu (ERBB2): breast cancer; poor prognosis. Trastuzumab (Herceptin) targets it → can cause cardiotoxicity
- JAK2: activated in polycythemia vera + essential thrombocytosis + myelofibrosis. PV = ↑RBCs (always) + ↑WBCs/Plt. Pruritus after shower (basophilia). Treat hyperviscosity with phlebotomy
- KRAS: first gene mutated in colonic polyps. Colon cancer sequence: KRAS → PTEN → DCC → TP53. Metastatic cancer → TP53
- MEN1: pituitary + parathyroid + pancreatic tumors (3Ps). MEN1 gene. NBME can give just ONE finding (e.g., gastrinoma alone) → still answer MEN1
- MSH2/6, MLH1, PMS2: HNPCC mismatch repair genes → microsatellite instability → colon + gynecologic cancers
- NF1: chromosome 17; AD. Café au lait spots + neurofibromas + axillary freckling + optic glioma. Classic variable expressivity
- NF2: chromosome 22; AD. Bilateral acoustic schwannoma + meningioma
- RB: retinoblastoma + osteosarcoma. Tumor suppressor; AD. RB protein represses E2F transcription. CDK/Cyclin phosphorylates RB → releases E2F → cell cycle. ↓ RB phosphorylation = wrong answer for cancer cells
- RET: proto-oncogene. MEN2A = pheo + medullary thyroid Ca + parathyroid adenoma. MEN2B = pheo + MTC + marfanoid habitus + mucosal neuromas
- TP53 (p53): halts cell cycle for DNA repair. Li-Fraumeni syndrome = congenital TP53 mutation. Metastatic cancer HY answer. Pleiotropy = one gene → multiple cancer types
- TSC1/2 (tuberous sclerosis): AD. Intellectual disability + periventricular tubers + adenoma sebaceum (angiofibromas) + subungual fibromas + cardiac rhabdomyoma (ball-in-valve diastolic rumble) + renal angiomyolipoma
- VHL: chromosome 3; AD. RCC + retinal hemangioblastoma + cerebellar hemangioblastoma + pancreatic cysts. RCC need not be bilateral
- WT1: Wilms tumor (nephroblastoma) = painless flank mass in 2-4yr old. Denys-Drash = gonadal agenesis + Wilms. WAGR = Wilms + Aniridia + GU abnormalities + Retardation. Beckwith-Wiedemann = macrosomia + macroglossia + hemihypertrophy + hypoglycemia + Wilms
- ATP7B: Wilson disease; AR; chromosome 13. Copper overload → ↑ urinary Cu + ↓ ceruloplasmin. Kayser-Fleischer rings (cornea). Basal ganglia (putamen) deposition. Young patient + Parkinsonism = Wilson until ruled out. Treat with penicillamine
- CFTR: cystic fibrosis; chromosome 7; AR. Cl- channel normally at cell surface; mutation → ER retention ('abnormal protein folding'). ΔF508 = most common mutation. Sweat Cl >60 mEq/L = diagnostic. Vitamin E deficiency HY in CF
- DMD: X-linked. Dystrophin mutation → disrupts α/β-dystroglycan. Pseudohypertrophy (fibroadipose replacing muscle). Duchenne = frameshift, Gower sign; Becker = non-frameshift, adolescent onset
- FBN1: Marfan; AD; chromosome 15. Fibrillin glycoprotein around elastin. MVP + aortic dissection risk. NOT associated with berry aneurysms (EDS and ADPKD are)
- FMR1: Fragile X; X-linked; CGG repeat. Hypermethylation → silencing. Large ears + macrorchidism + long jaw + intellectual disability in boys
- FXN: Friedreich ataxia; GAA repeat. Ataxia + scoliosis + cardiomyopathy + ↓ reflexes
- HFE: hereditary hemochromatosis; AR; chromosome 6. ↑ intestinal iron absorption. Bronze diabetes (hyperpigmentation + DM). Pseudogout in hemochromatosis. ↑ HCC risk. Treat with phlebotomy (not chelation, chelation = secondary hemochromatosis from transfusions)
- HTT: Huntington; AD; CAG repeat; chromosome 4. Chorea + dementia + psychiatric sx in 30s-40s. Caudate nucleus affected. Anticipation (earlier each generation)
- PKD1/2: ADPKD; AD. Adult onset (30s-40s). ↑ BP from ↑ RAAS (cysts compress microvasculature). Berry aneurysm risk → SAH. Serial BP checks correct over MRA screening (MRA only if FHx of SAH)
- PKHD1: ARPKD; AR. Pediatric cystic kidneys. Associated with hepatic fibrosis

## GI (13)

- H. pylori: urease → NH3 → survives stomach acid. MC cause of PUD worldwide. ↑ gastric cancer risk 3-6x + MALT lymphoma. Test: urea breath test or stool Ag (non-invasive); biopsy if active disease
- Duodenal ulcer: pain relieved by eating. Gastric ulcer: pain worsened by eating. ALWAYS biopsy gastric ulcer (↑ malignancy risk)
- Zollinger-Ellison: gastrinoma → ↑↑ acid → multiple recurrent ulcers (distal duodenum/jejunum) + secretory diarrhea. 25% have MEN1. Secretin stimulation test (paradoxical ↑ gastrin)
- Crohn vs UC: Crohn = transmural, any segment, skip lesions, granulomas, perianal disease, smoking ↑ risk, rectal SPARING. UC = mucosal only, continuous, rectum ALWAYS involved, no fistulas, smoking PROTECTIVE
- UC: crypt abscesses = pathognomonic histology. p-ANCA+ (vs ASCA+ in Crohn). PSC association (UC > Crohn). Toxic megacolon: colon >6cm + systemic toxicity → emergency. UC curable by colectomy
- Cirrhosis: hepatic stellate cell → TGF-β → collagen. SBP: PMN >250/μL in ascites → cefotaxime. Hepatic encephalopathy: lactulose + rifaximin. Variceal bleed: octreotide + endoscopic banding. β-blockers: prevent variceal bleed
- HBV serology: HBsAg+ = infected. Anti-HBs+ = immune (vaccination or recovery). HBeAg+ = highly infectious. Anti-HBc IgM = acute. Window period = only anti-HBc IgM+. HCV: IVDU = MC transmission; treat with NS5A/NS5B inhibitors
- Acute pancreatitis: GET SMASHED (Gallstones MC, Ethanol 2nd, Trauma, Steroids, Mumps, Autoimmune, Scorpion, Hypercalcemia/Hypertriglyceridemia, ERCP, Drugs). Lipase more specific than amylase
- Barrett esophagus: squamous → intestinal columnar (goblet cells) from GERD. Stomach has mucous neck cells (foveolar) not goblet cells. ↑ risk adenocarcinoma
- Colon cancer sequence: KRAS → PTEN → DCC → TP53. Lynch syndrome (HNPCC) = mismatch repair gene mutations. FAP = APC mutation = 100% cancer risk
- Wilson disease: ↑ Cu in liver + brain (putamen) + cornea (Kayser-Fleischer). ↑ urine Cu + ↓ ceruloplasmin. Young Parkinsonism = Wilson. Penicillamine. Liver transplant curative
- Hemochromatosis (HFE): ↑ intestinal iron absorption. Bronze diabetes (skin + DM). Pseudogout association. ↑ HCC risk. Ferritin >300 diagnostic. Phlebotomy (NOT deferoxamine, that's for transfusional siderosis)
- Budd-Chiari syndrome: hepatic vein thrombosis → painful hepatomegaly + ascites ± jaundice. Associated with polycythemia vera, pregnancy, OCP, hypercoagulable states

## HEME (14)

- Iron deficiency: ↓ ferritin (earliest marker), ↑ TIBC, ↓ serum iron, microcytic hypochromic. Pica (ice = pagophagia). Plummer-Vinson = IDA + esophageal web + atrophic glossitis → ↑ esophageal SCC
- ACD: ↑ ferritin (iron trapped in stores), ↓ TIBC, ↓ serum iron. Hepcidin ↑ (from IL-6) → ↓ ferroportin. MC anemia in hospitalized patients
- B12 deficiency: ↑ MMA + ↑ homocysteine. Neurologic sx (posterior + lateral columns). Pernicious anemia = MC cause (anti-IF Ab + anti-parietal cell Ab). Fish tapeworm (D. latum). NEVER give folate alone (worsens neuropathy)
- Folate deficiency: ↑ homocysteine only, NORMAL MMA. NO neurologic symptoms. MC cause in alcoholics. Neural tube defects (supplement periconceptionally)
- G6PD deficiency: XLR. Oxidative stress (infection MC trigger, primaquine, dapsone, fava beans) → Heinz bodies + bite cells. Test during RECOVERY (false normal during crisis, old G6PD-deficient RBCs already destroyed)
- Hereditary spherocytosis: AD (spectrin/ankyrin deficiency). ↑ MCHC (unique). Negative Coombs. Pigment gallstones. Aplastic crisis with Parvovirus B19. Splenectomy curative
- Sickle cell: HbS (Glu→Val on β-chain). Functional asplenia by age 5 → vaccinate (pneumococcal + meningococcal + Hib) + penicillin prophylaxis. Hydroxyurea → ↑ HbF. Parvovirus B19 → aplastic crisis. Acute chest syndrome = MC cause of death
- Thalassemia: β-thal minor: ↑ HbA2 >3.5% (diagnostic). β-thal major: transfusion-dependent, iron overload → chelation (deferoxamine). Hair-on-end skull X-ray. α-thal: Southeast Asian; 4 gene deletions = hydrops fetalis (fatal)
- DIC: ALL coags abnormal (↑PT + ↑PTT + ↓Plt + ↓fibrinogen + ↑D-dimer). APL + DIC = classic → ATRA treats leukemia AND DIC. TTP = ↓Plt + schistocytes but NORMAL PT/PTT/fibrinogen
- TTP: anti-ADAMTS13. Pentad: thrombocytopenia + MAHA + neuro sx + fever + renal dysfunction. EMERGENT plasma exchange. Do NOT transfuse platelets
- HIT: anti-PF4-heparin. Platelet drop 30-50% at 5-10 days on heparin → paradoxical THROMBOSIS. Stop ALL heparin. Switch to argatroban/bivalirudin. Do NOT start warfarin until Plt >150k
- Hemophilia A (F8) vs B (F9): both XLR. ↑ PTT, normal PT, normal Plt. Hemarthroses. Hemophilia A: DDAVP (mild) or F8 concentrate. Christmas disease = Hemophilia B
- vWD: MC inherited bleeding disorder. Mucocutaneous bleeding. ↑ PTT, ↑ bleeding time. Ristocetin fails to aggregate platelets (except type 2B where it's paradoxically ↑). DDAVP for type 1. Contraindicated in type 2B
- Parvovirus B19: aplastic crisis in chronic hemolytic anemias (sickle cell, spherocytosis). Erythema infectiosum (fifth disease) in children. Hydrops fetalis in utero

## IMMUNO (16)

- Hypersensitivity Type I: IgE crosslinks on mast cells → histamine + tryptase. Late phase: eosinophils. Examples: anaphylaxis, atopy, asthma. Tx: epinephrine IM first
- Hypersensitivity Type II: Ab against cells/tissues/receptors. Examples: Graves, Hashimoto, Goodpasture, pernicious anemia, HIT, ITP, MG, Lambert-Eaton, bullous pemphigoid, pemphigus, rheumatic fever
- Hypersensitivity Type III: Ab-Ag complexes in vessels. Examples: PSGN, SLE, PAN, serum sickness, Arthus reaction. ↓ C3, ↓ C4 = complement consumption
- Hypersensitivity Type IV: T-cell mediated (no Ab). Examples: PPD test, contact dermatitis (nickel = MC allergen), graft-vs-host, granulomas, T1DM
- SCID: ADA deficiency (AR) or γc chain deficiency (XLR). No adaptive immunity. Absent thymic shadow on CXR. Maternal T-cells → GvHD. No live vaccines. HSCT curative
- DiGeorge (22q11.2): CATCH-22. Cardiac defects + Abnormal facies + Thymic aplasia + Cleft palate + Hypocalcemia (↓ PTH). Low T-cells, normal B-cells. Neonatal hypocalcemia + cardiac defect = DiGeorge until ruled out
- Bruton XLA: BTK mutation. B-cell arrest at pre-B stage. No tonsils/lymph nodes. Recurrent encapsulated bacteria after 6 months. IVIG treatment
- CVID: most symptomatic primary Ab deficiency in adults. B-cells present but non-functional. Bimodal age. Giardia + sinopulmonary infections + ↑ lymphoma risk + ↑ gastric cancer
- Selective IgA deficiency: MC primary immunodeficiency (1:700). Anaphylaxis with blood transfusion (anti-IgA Ab). Use IgA-deficient blood or washed RBCs. Celiac screening: use tTG-IgG (not IgA-based tests)
- Hyper-IgM: CD40L mutation (XLR). ↑↑ IgM, absent IgG/IgA/IgE. PCP + encapsulated bacteria + Cryptosporidium cholangitis. IVIG + TMP-SMX prophylaxis
- Wiskott-Aldrich: WASp mutation (XLR). Triad: Thrombocytopenia (SMALL platelets) + Eczema + Recurrent infections. ↑ IgA/IgE, ↓ IgM. HSCT curative
- CGD: NADPH oxidase defect → can't kill catalase+ organisms. Organisms: S. aureus, Aspergillus, Serratia, Nocardia, Burkholderia. NBT test (no color change). DHR flow cytometry diagnostic
- Chediak-Higashi: LYST mutation (AR). Silvery hair + giant neutrophil granules + partial albinism + recurrent staph. Accelerated phase = HLH (EBV-triggered). HSCT curative
- C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency (hereditary angioedema): AD. Non-urticarial angioedema. No response to epinephrine. ↓ C4 always low (even between attacks). Tx: C1-INH concentrate, icatibant, or FFP
- Terminal complement (C5-C9) deficiency: recurrent Neisseria meningitidis AND gonorrhoeae. Check CH50. Vaccinate against Neisseria
- GvHD: donor T-cells attack host. Acute (<100 days): skin → GI → liver. Chronic (>100 days): scleroderma-like. Irradiate blood for immunocompromised patients

## MSK (9)

- Osteoporosis: ↑ RANKL → ↑ osteoclasts. Estrogen ↑ OPG (decoy receptor for RANKL) → ↓ osteoclasts. Post-menopausal ↓ estrogen → ↑ bone resorption. Bisphosphonates = first-line. Glucocorticoids = MC drug cause secondary osteoporosis
- OA: wear and tear. DIP (Heberden nodes) + PIP (Bouchard nodes) in hands. RA spares DIP. No systemic inflammation in OA (normal ESR, CRP)
- Gout: NEEDLE-shaped, NEGATIVELY birefringent crystals (yellow under parallel polarized light). 1st MTP = podagra. Treat acute: NSAIDs/colchicine/steroids. Prevent: allopurinol (xanthine oxidase inhibitor) START AFTER acute resolved
- Pseudogout: RHOMBOID-shaped, POSITIVELY birefringent crystals (blue under parallel). Wrist + knee (large joints). Chondrocalcinosis on X-ray. Associated with hyperparathyroidism + hemochromatosis + hypothyroidism
- Ankylosing spondylitis: HLA-B27 (>90%). Young males. Bamboo spine. Morning stiffness >1hr, IMPROVES with exercise (unlike mechanical back pain). Anterior uveitis. Sacroiliac joint involvement
- RA: symmetric MCP + PIP (spares DIP). Morning stiffness >1hr. Ulnar deviation + Bouchard nodes. Anti-CCP = more specific than RF. Screen for latent TB before anti-TNF. C1-C2 subluxation (flex X-ray before intubation)
- Felty syndrome = RA + neutropenia + splenomegaly. Sjögren primary associations: anti-SS-A/Ro + anti-SS-B/La. Anti-centromere = CREST. Anti-Scl-70 = diffuse scleroderma
- Septic arthritis: MC cause = N. gonorrhoeae (sexually active young adults). S. aureus = MC non-gonococcal. Knee = MC joint in adults. Joint aspiration WBC >50k PMN = diagnostic. Never delay antibiotics
- Duchenne: frameshift mutation (dystrophin). Pseudohypertrophy. Gower maneuver. X-linked. Becker = non-frameshift, less severe, adolescent onset

## NEURO (14)

- Alzheimer: Aβ plaques + tau tangles → ↓ cholinergic neurons (nucleus basalis of Meynert). APOE ε4 = ↑ risk. Down syndrome (trisomy 21) → APP overexpression → dementia by 40. Treat with donepezil + memantine
- Parkinson: loss of SNpc dopaminergic neurons. Lewy bodies (α-synuclein). TRAP (resting Tremor, Rigidity, Akinesia, Postural instability). REM sleep behavior disorder = earliest non-motor sx. Anosmia = early prodromal
- MS: periventricular white matter plaques (Dawson fingers). Oligoclonal IgG bands in CSF (not serum). Optic neuritis = MC first presentation. Uhthoff phenomenon = sx worse with heat. Treat relapse with IV methylprednisolone
- Huntington: AD, CAG repeat, chromosome 4. Caudate nucleus. Chorea + dementia + psychiatric sx. Anticipation. Suicide risk high on disclosure
- GBS: ascending flaccid paralysis + areflexia. Albuminocytologic dissociation (↑ protein, normal WBC in CSF). MC trigger = Campylobacter jejuni. Treat with IVIG or plasmapheresis (NOT steroids). Monitor FVC
- MCA stroke: contralateral hemiplegia (arm > leg) + sensory loss + hemianopia ± aphasia (dominant) or neglect (non-dominant). tPA ≤4.5 hrs. Thrombectomy ≤6-24 hrs for LVO
- SAH: 'worst headache of life.' Berry aneurysm rupture. CT-negative → LP (xanthochromia). Nimodipine for vasospasm. AComm = MC aneurysm. PComm = CN III palsy (complete = surgical urgency)
- Meningitis empiric treatment: ceftriaxone + vancomycin + dexamethasone. Add ampicillin for Listeria (elderly, immunocompromised, neonates). Dex BEFORE or WITH first antibiotic dose
- Bacterial meningitis organism by age: Neonates = GBS + E. coli + Listeria. 5-30yo = N. meningitidis. >30 = S. pneumoniae (MC overall in adults). Elderly/immunocompromised = Listeria
- Drug-induced parkinsonism: DA blockers (antipsychotics, metoclopramide) → reversible parkinsonism. Levodopa/carbidopa: carbidopa = peripheral decarboxylase inhibitor
- Wilson disease (ATP7B): young patient + Parkinsonism = Wilson until ruled out. Kayser-Fleischer rings (slit lamp exam). Copper deposits in putamen. ↑ urine Cu + ↓ ceruloplasmin. Penicillamine chelation
- Migraine: CSD → trigeminal activation → CGRP release. Treat acute with triptans (5-HT1B/1D). Prevention: propranolol, topiramate, amitriptyline, anti-CGRP mAbs. Avoid opioids (medication overuse headache)
- ADPKD (PKD1/2): adult polycystic kidneys. Berry aneurysm risk. Serial BP monitoring (NOT MRA screening unless FHx SAH). Polycystin protein
- Normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH): Wacky + Wobbly + Wet (dementia + gait ataxia + urinary incontinence). Treat with VP shunt

## PATH (48)

- Anaplasia = reversion to immature cell type = high-grade cancer = poor prognosis
- Aplasia = organ fails to develop (DiGeorge → thymus + parathyroid aplasia)
- Atrophy = ↓ cell size/number from under-stimulation (orchiectomy → prostate atrophy)
- Dysplasia = reversible cellular atypia; precursor to cancer; NOT yet cancerous; can spontaneously regress
- Hyperplasia = ↑ cell number (endometrial hyperplasia from unopposed estrogen = HY)
- Hypertrophy = ↑ cell size (high afterload → concentric; high preload → eccentric LVH)
- Metaplasia = one mature cell → another mature cell; reversible. Barrett = squamous → intestinal columnar (has goblet cells)
- Neoplasia = irreversible tumorigenic conversion. CIN = technically dysplasia despite name; often reversible
- Apoptosis = programmed, ATP-requiring. Intrinsic: cytochrome c → apoptosome → caspases. Extrinsic: FAS/TNF-R → DISC → caspases
- Hepatocyte death in hepatitis = T-cell-mediated apoptosis via extrinsic pathway; NOT direct viral cytopathicity
- Apoptotic cells → apoptotic bodies → phagocytosed. DNA laddering at 180bp in apoptosis
- Menstruation = apoptosis. Menopause = atrophy. Do not confuse
- Coagulative necrosis: architecture maintained. Seen in MI + ATN
- Liquefactive necrosis: architecture not maintained. ALL CNS infarcts + ALL abscesses
- Caseous necrosis: cheese-like. TB + fungal + Bartonella henselae (cat-scratch disease)
- Enzymatic fat necrosis: acute pancreatitis → lipases + Ca2+ saponification (soap formation)
- Non-enzymatic fat necrosis: breast trauma
- Fibrinoid necrosis: looks like fibrin but isn't; seen in polyarteritis nodosa
- Dry gangrene = coagulative necrosis from ischemia (e.g., diabetic foot)
- Wet gangrene = infective limb necrosis. Fournier = perineal wet gangrene in DM
- Gas gangrene = C. perfringens α-toxin/phospholipase → CO2 gas → crepitus (subcutaneous emphysema)
- Cystic medial necrosis: large artery collagen defect → aortic dissection. Seen in Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos, HTN
- Red neurons = acute CNS ischemia on H&E (eosinophilic). No histo changes = immediate post-MI finding
- Microglia = CNS resident macrophages. Astrocytes = CNS scar formation (gliosis)
- Wallerian degeneration = axon/myelin degeneration DISTAL to injury. PNS regenerates (Schwann cells); CNS does NOT (oligodendrocytes)
- Optic nerve = CNS extension; myelinated by oligodendrocytes → permanent damage. Other CNs = PNS (Schwann) → can recover
- Reversible cell injury: cellular swelling, blebbing, ↓ ATP, fatty change. Swelling = ↓ Na/K-ATPase activity → Na+ buildup → H2O retention
- Irreversible cell injury: karyolysis (nucleus dissolves), karyorrhexis (nucleus fragments), pyknosis (nucleus condenses/shrinks), plasma membrane rupture
- Dystrophic calcification = calcium in necrotic tissue (irreversible). Metastatic calcification = calcium deposition in normal tissue from hypercalcemia (reversible)
- Neutrophils = cell of acute inflammation. First responders 0-48hrs. NADPH oxidase → H2O2; MPO → HOCl (bleach). Pus = dead neutrophils
- Macrophages = cell of chronic inflammation. Predominate after 48hrs. APCs → present to CD4+ T cells
- TNF-α → ↑ vascular permeability. IL-1 → fever. IL-6 → acute phase proteins (CRP). IL-12 → stimulates CD4+ T cells
- Leukemoid reaction = WBC >30, 000 = reactive neutrophilia. Looks like leukemia but smear shows neutrophils not leukemic blasts
- Leukocyte extravasation: Selectins (rolling) → LFA-1/CD18 (tight binding; deficient in LAD) → PECAM-1 (diapedesis) → LTB4/IL-8/C5a (migration)
- Non-caseating granulomas in sarcoid secrete 1α-hydroxylase → ↑ calcitriol → hypercalcemia
- Granuloma contents: epithelioid macrophages (aka histiocytes) + Langhans giant cells
- TGF-β → fibroblast proliferation + collagen. PDGF → fibroblast recruitment. VEGF → angiogenesis (stimulated by ↓pO2 in tumors). FGF → fibroblast proliferation. EGF → epithelial re-epithelialization
- Wound healing: Phase 1 = hemostasis (platelets + coagulation). Phase 2 = inflammation (neutrophils first 0-48h, then macrophages). Phase 3 = proliferation (type III collagen = pink = weak). Phase 4 = remodeling (type III → type I collagen = white = strong; 80% tensile strength restored)
- Type I collagen: bone. Type II: cartilage + discs + vitreous. Type III: blood vessels + early wounds. Type IV: basement membranes
- Osteogenesis imperfecta = Type I collagen defect → fractures, blue sclerae, conductive hearing loss
- Ehlers-Danlos (vascular) = Type III collagen defect → hyperextensible joints, aortic dissection, berry aneurysms
- Alport syndrome = Type IV collagen mutation (XR) → hematuria + eye + ear problems in males
- Goodpasture = Anti-Type IV collagen Ab → hemoptysis + hematuria; linear IF on biopsy
- Stage vs Grade: Stage = spread (TNM). Grade = mitoses + anaplasia. STAGE > grade for prognosis on USMLE
- In situ = Stage 0 = superficial to basement membrane. Microinvasion = crossed basement membrane → stage ↑
- Carcinoma = epithelial origin. Sarcoma = mesenchymal/connective tissue. Blastoma = immature cells. Glioma = glial cells. Melanoma = melanocytes
- Shock types: Cardiogenic (↓CO, ↑SVR, ↑PCWP). Hypovolemic (↓CO, ↑SVR, ↓PCWP). Septic (↑CO early, ↓SVR, ↓PCWP). Neurogenic (↓CO, ↓SVR, ↓PCWP)
- Cardiogenic shock = ↑ PCWP = HY distinction. Distributive shock = septic + anaphylactic + neurogenic

## PHARM (15)

- Imatinib: BCR-ABL TKI for CML. Can cause fluid retention/peripheral edema
- ATRA (all-trans retinoic acid): differentiates APL (M3) → treats leukemia AND DIC simultaneously
- Trastuzumab (Herceptin): HER2/neu targeting → cardiotoxicity. Rituximab: anti-CD20 → CLL, NHL, membranous GN (off-label)
- Erlotinib: EGFR TKI for lung adenocarcinoma. Vismodegib: Hedgehog pathway inhibitor for BCC
- Penicillamine: copper chelation for Wilson disease. Deferoxamine/deferasirox: iron chelation for transfusional siderosis (β-thal major). NOT used in hereditary hemochromatosis (phlebotomy used instead)
- Hydroxyurea: ↑ HbF → ↓ sickling in sickle cell. Also used in PV/essential thrombocytosis (↓ recurrence)
- Ivacaftor (potentiator) + lumacaftor/tezacaftor (correctors): CF treatment. Improve Cl- channel function, location, and structure
- DDAVP (desmopressin): releases vWF from Weibel-Palade bodies → useful for type 1 vWD and mild hemophilia A. CONTRAINDICATED in vWD type 2B
- Colchicine: acute gout treatment (within 36hrs). Allopurinol/febuxostat: xanthine oxidase inhibitors for chronic gout prevention. Start AFTER acute episode resolves. Cover with colchicine when starting allopurinol
- Dupilumab (anti-IL4Rα): moderate-severe atopic dermatitis. Omalizumab (anti-IgE): severe allergic asthma. Mepolizumab (anti-IL5): severe eosinophilic asthma
- Nimodipine: Ca-channel blocker for vasospasm prevention in SAH (NOT for BP control, doesn't significantly lower BP). Give for 21 days after SAH
- SGLT-2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin): ↓ CV mortality + ↓ HF hospitalizations + ↓ CKD progression in DM. Now recommended in HFrEF and HFpEF
- GLP-1 RAs (semaglutide, liraglutide): ↓ CV events + weight loss in T2DM. Tirzepatide = dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist
- Metformin: first-line T2DM. Stop at GFR <30 (lactic acidosis risk). ↓ hepatic gluconeogenesis. Weight neutral, no hypoglycemia
- TMP-SMX: covers PCP prophylaxis (CD4 <200) AND Toxoplasmosis prophylaxis (CD4 <100). At CD4 <50: add azithromycin weekly for MAC prophylaxis

## PSYCH (9)

- Schizophrenia duration: brief psychotic <1 mo, schizophreniform 1-6 mo, schizophrenia ≥6 mo. Auditory hallucinations = MC positive sx. 4 DA pathways: mesolimbic (positive sx), mesocortical (negative/cognitive sx), nigrostriatal (EPS), tuberoinfundibular (prolactin)
- Clozapine: treatment-resistant schizophrenia. Monitor for agranulocytosis (weekly CBC). EPS: acute dystonia → benztropine. Akathisia → propranolol. Tardive dyskinesia → valbenazine/deutetrabenazine
- MDD: SIG E CAPS. Rule out hypothyroidism (TSH) and bipolar. SSRI = first line. Suicidal risk ↑ in first weeks of SSRI in adolescents. SSRI discontinuation syndrome = FINISH (Flu-like, Insomnia, Nausea, Imbalance, Sensory zapping, Hyperarousal)
- Bipolar I: ≥1 manic episode ≥7 days. NEVER give SSRI alone (precipitates mania). Lithium = best for suicide prevention; monitor TSH, BMP, Cr. Valproate = teratogenic (NTD). Lamotrigine = best for bipolar depression; SJS risk with rapid titration
- PTSD: ≥1 month. Acute stress disorder = same sx <1 month with more dissociation. SSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine) = only FDA-approved pharmacotherapy. Prolonged Exposure or CPT = first-line therapy. Prazosin = nightmares. AVOID benzos
- OCD: ego-DYSTONIC (vs OCPD = ego-SYNTONIC). First-line: CBT with ERP + high-dose SSRI. PANDAS = strep-triggered OCD in children. Caudate nucleus dysfunction
- Alcohol withdrawal timeline: 6-24h = tremors/autonomic hyperactivity. 12-48h = seizures. 48-72h = delirium tremens (LIFE-THREATENING). Tx = benzodiazepines (CIWA-Ar). Give THIAMINE BEFORE glucose (Wernicke prevention)
- Opioid overdose triad: coma + prompt respiratory depression + miosis. Naloxone = antidote. MAT: methadone (full agonist) or buprenorphine/naloxone (Suboxone, partial agonist). Withdrawal = not life-threatening (unlike alcohol/benzos)
- Delusional disorder: non-bizarre delusions, functioning preserved, ≥1 month. Ekbom syndrome (delusional parasitosis) = matchbox sign → presents to dermatologist. Tx: antipsychotics; do NOT agree with delusion

## PULM (8)

- COPD: smoking = 95%. Blue bloater (chronic bronchitis: ↑ CO2, ↑ RBC, edema) vs Pink puffer (emphysema: barrel chest, pursed lips, cachexia). α1-AT deficiency: panacinar lower lobe emphysema. Smoking cessation = only disease-modifying intervention
- Asthma: Th2/eosinophilic inflammation. Samter triad = asthma + nasal polyps + aspirin sensitivity (COX-1 → ↓PGE2 → ↑ leukotrienes). SABA rescue → ICS controller → LABA+ICS. Severe = Mg2+ IV + heliox
- PE: dyspnea (MC sx) + tachycardia (MC sign). S1Q3T3 (only 20%). CT-PA = gold standard. Factor V Leiden = MC inherited thrombophilia. Massive PE = thrombolysis (tPA) or embolectomy. PERC rule excludes PE if pretest probability low
- Lung cancer: adenocarcinoma = MC overall + MC in never-smokers. Small cell = no surgery, chemo only. Squamous cell → PTHrp (hypercalcemia). Small cell → SIADH + ACTH + LEMS + anti-Hu cerebellar ataxia. LEMS: ↑ strength with repetition (vs MG ↓)
- TB: upper lobe cavitary lesion. RIPE × 2 months → RI × 4 months. INH toxicity: hepatotoxicity + peripheral neuropathy (prevent with B6/pyridoxine). Screen for latent TB (IGRA) before anti-TNF therapy
- Asbestosis: ferruginous bodies + pleural plaques on CXR (soft tissue densities). Macrophage = cell that initiates pulmonary fibrosis. Mesothelioma = mesothelial cells from asbestos. Shipyard workers, electricians, construction workers
- Sarcoidosis: non-caseating granulomas + bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy + ↑ ACE + hypercalcemia (from granuloma 1α-hydroxylase). AA 10-17x higher risk. Löfgren syndrome = BHL + erythema nodosum + periarthritis = good prognosis
- CF (CFTR): ΔF508 = MC mutation. Abnormal protein folding (ER retention). Sweat Cl >60 = diagnostic. Pseudomonas > S. aureus after age 10. Fat-soluble vitamin malabsorption. Vitamin E deficiency = neuropathy in CF

## RENAL (9)

- Nephrotic: massive proteinuria (>3.5g/day) + hypoalbuminemia + edema + hyperlipidemia + hypercoagulability. MCD = MC in children (EM shows foot process effacement; normal LM). Diabetic nephropathy = MC in adults (KW nodules)
- FSGS = MC in African Americans. Collapsing variant = HIV (HIVAN). Membranous = anti-PLA2R Ab (primary); HBV/SLE/malignancy. Renal vein thrombosis risk highest in membranous
- Nephritic: hematuria + RBC casts (pathognomonic) + HTN + oliguria + ↑ Cr. PSGN: ↓C3 ↓C4 after strep infection (10-14 days AFTER). IgA nephropathy: hematuria CONCURRENT with URI
- Goodpasture: anti-GBM Ab (Type IV collagen α3 chain). Pulmonary hemorrhage + nephritis. Linear IF on renal biopsy. Young man + hemoptysis + hematuria. Plasmapheresis + immunosuppression
- ANCA vasculitis: GPA = c-ANCA/PR3 (Water Closet mnemonic). MPA = p-ANCA/MPO. Pauci-immune (no immune complexes) = ANCA-associated GN
- AKI: Pre-renal (FENa <1%, BUN/Cr >20, concentrated urine). ATN (FENa >2%, muddy brown casts; ischemia or nephrotoxins). Drug-induced AIN (WBC casts + eosinophiluria + rash + eosinophilia). Post-renal (BPH in elderly men)
- Rhabdomyolysis: ↑ CPK + myoglobinuria (urine dipstick+ for blood but NO RBCs on microscopy). ATN from myoglobin. Causes: crush, seizures, statins, alcohol, cocaine
- CKD: DM = MC cause ESRD (worldwide). HTN = 2nd MC. CKD-MBD: ↓ calcitriol → ↑ PTH → ↑ PO4 → secondary hyperparathyroidism. EPO → normocytic anemia. ACEi/ARBs slow progression. SGLT2i now recommended
- ADPKD: PKD1/PKD2. Adult. Hypertension (↑ RAAS from cyst compression). ↑ risk berry aneurysm/SAH. Most common extra-renal cysts: liver (85% by age 30)

## REPRO (11)

- Ectopic pregnancy: fallopian tube (ampulla MC). Amenorrhea + vaginal bleeding + pelvic pain triad. β-hCG not doubling (normal doubles every 48hrs). Ruptured = surgical emergency. Unruptured + stable → methotrexate
- Preeclampsia: new HTN >140/90 + proteinuria after 20 wks. Severe: SBP >160 + thrombocytopenia <100k + ↑ LFTs + renal failure. HELLP = Hemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes + Low Platelets. Delivery = definitive treatment. Magnesium = seizure prophylaxis
- Gestational DM: 1-hr 50g GCT screen at 24-28 wks. If ≥140 → 3-hr OGTT. Macrosomia → neonatal hypoglycemia. 7x ↑ T2DM risk for mother. Postpartum 75g OGTT at 4-12 weeks
- PCOS: LH:FSH ratio >2:1. ↑ androgens + anovulation + polycystic ovaries (Rotterdam criteria). Insulin resistance → acanthosis nigricans. ↑ risk endometrial cancer (unopposed estrogen). Tx: OCPs + metformin + clomiphene for fertility
- Endometriosis: ectopic endometrium → chocolate cysts (ovarian endometriomas). Dysmenorrhea + dyspareunia + dyschezia + infertility. Retrograde menstruation theory. CA-125 ↑ but nonspecific. Laparoscopy = definitive diagnosis
- Cervical cancer: HPV 16/18. E6 inhibits p53, E7 inhibits RB. Koilocytes on Pap smear. Postcoital bleeding = MC symptom. Pap screen starts at age 21 regardless of sexual activity. HPV vaccine = Gardasil 9
- Breast cancer: BRCA1 → triple-negative. BRCA2 → ER+. HER2+ = poor prognosis. Trastuzumab targets HER2 → cardiotoxicity. ER+/PR+: tamoxifen (premenopausal) or aromatase inhibitors (postmenopausal). IBC = inflammatory breast cancer (peau d'orange + erythema, NOT a palpable mass)
- Postpartum depression: 10-15% of postpartum women. Brexanolone (allopregnanolone) = IV treatment specifically for PPD. Postpartum psychosis = psychiatric emergency (infanticide risk) → hospitalize
- BPH: DHT-mediated (transition zone). Tamsulosin (α-blocker, rapid symptom relief) + finasteride (5α-reductase inhibitor, shrinks gland). MC cause post-renal AKI in men
- Testicular cancer: MC solid tumor in males 15-35. Painless testicular mass. Seminoma: radiosensitive, AFP NEVER elevated (only β-hCG). Non-seminoma: AFP + β-hCG. Para-aortic LN drainage (not inguinal). Orchiectomy = inguinal approach (never scrotal biopsy)
- Prostate cancer: MC cancer in men. AA 2x higher risk. Osteoblastic metastases (bone scan positive). PSA = screening but controversial. Gleason grading. ADT = GnRH agonists (leuprolide = medical castration)
