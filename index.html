THE PROBLEM

Why you feel stuck.

You are not lazy. You are not dumb. You got into medical school on merit. What broke is your relationship with the material. Five patterns we see in every student we work with.

01

You recognize buzzwords instead of mechanisms.

You learned "stones, bones, groans" but cannot draw the PTH cascade. When the NBME drops the buzzword, you guess.

02

You memorize without discriminating.

When two diseases share six of seven symptoms, lists do not help. You need the single lab value or timeline that splits them.

03

Your knowledge lives in fragments.

Cardio Monday, renal Tuesday. The exam asks how a heart failure drug affects kidney electrolytes. Fragmented facts shatter under pressure.

04

Your error log is dead or never started.

You miss the same kind of question over and over. Nothing changes because you never wrote down the pattern, only the answer.

05

You are running on cortisol.

Anxiety steals working memory. ADHD steals attention. Burnout steals motivation. No amount of UWorld questions fixes a fried nervous system.

INSIDE THE SYSTEM

Stop memorizing. See the pattern.

If you rely on symptoms, you get trapped. If you rely on the discriminator, you pass. Here is what mechanism-first reasoning looks like in practice.

◈ EXAMPLE: THE MASTER PIVOT

The Trap (Noise)

The NBME shows you a patient with wheezing, chronic shortness of breath, and a low FEV1/FVC ratio. If you memorize symptoms, Asthma and COPD look identical.

The Discriminator (Signal)

Ignore the overlap. Hunt for the DLCO. Normal or increased in Asthma. Decreased in COPD emphysema, because the alveolar wall is destroyed.

One lab value collapses the differential.

Will this replace UWorld or Amboss?
No. BBB is the reasoning layer that sits on top of your Qbank. UWorld gives you 4000 questions. We give you the framework to answer all of them faster and more accurately. Use both.
I am a US-IMG or FMG. Is this for me?
It was built for you. The founder is a third-year US-IMG who designed this system after grinding through CBSE, COMP, and Step 1 prep with no institutional safety net. IMG-specific scheduling and re-taker workflows are baked in.
Does this work for COMLEX as well as USMLE?
The mechanism-first method maps directly to both. COMLEX Level 1 tests the same physiology and pharmacology. Only OMM is unique, and BBB does not cover that yet.
How is this different from First Aid or Pathoma?
First Aid is a reference. Pathoma is a lecture series. BBB is a reasoning protocol. We do not compete with them. We teach you how to use them under exam pressure.
