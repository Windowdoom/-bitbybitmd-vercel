# Bit 20 Sampler — 20 NBME-Style Questions

## Q1

A 32-year-old with chronic wheezing and reduced FEV1/FVC undergoes PFTs. Which finding distinguishes asthma from COPD?

- A. Bronchodilator reversibility
- B. Decreased DLCO
- C ✅. Normal or increased DLCO
- D. Hyperinflation on CXR

**Answer: C.** Normal or increased DLCO

**Pivot:** DLCO splits asthma from emphysematous COPD.

**Explanation:** Both obstruct airflow and wheeze. The discriminator is the alveolar membrane. Intact in asthma so DLCO is normal or increased. Destroyed in emphysema so DLCO drops. Reversibility is supportive but less specific.

## Q2

Hyponatremia with urine osm 600 and serum osm 260. Diagnosis?

- A. Diabetes insipidus
- B ✅. SIADH
- C. Psychogenic polydipsia
- D. Cerebral salt wasting

**Answer: B.** SIADH

**Pivot:** Concentrated urine plus dilute serum equals SIADH.

**Explanation:** ADH is inappropriately high. Urine stays concentrated while serum dilutes. DI is the mirror image.

## Q3

24-year-old. Skip lesions, transmural inflammation, granulomas on colonoscopy. Diagnosis?

- A. Ulcerative colitis
- B ✅. Crohn disease
- C. Microscopic colitis
- D. C. difficile

**Answer: B.** Crohn disease

**Pivot:** Transmural plus skip lesions equals Crohn.

**Explanation:** UC is continuous, mucosal, rectal. Crohn skips, full thickness, can affect mouth to anus. Granulomas seal the diagnosis.

## Q4

Ascending paralysis 2 weeks after Campylobacter. CSF: high protein, normal cell count. Diagnosis?

- A. Multiple sclerosis
- B ✅. Guillain-Barré
- C. Myasthenia gravis
- D. Botulism

**Answer: B.** Guillain-Barré

**Pivot:** Albuminocytologic dissociation equals GBS.

**Explanation:** GBS hits the PNS. MS hits the CNS and shows oligoclonal bands. The CSF pattern with high protein and no cells is classic for GBS.

## Q5

55-year-old. Chest pain. Troponin 0.8. ST depressions on ECG. Diagnosis?

- A. Stable angina
- B. Unstable angina
- C ✅. NSTEMI
- D. STEMI

**Answer: C.** NSTEMI

**Pivot:** Troponin separates unstable angina from NSTEMI.

**Explanation:** Unstable angina and NSTEMI present identically. Troponin is the splitter. Positive equals NSTEMI. STEMI requires ST elevation.

## Q6

HTN with low renin and high aldosterone. Diagnosis?

- A ✅. Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn)
- B. Renal artery stenosis
- C. Cushing
- D. Pheochromocytoma

**Answer: A.** Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn)

**Pivot:** Aldosterone over renin ratio splits primary from secondary.

**Explanation:** Conn is autonomous, so renin is suppressed. Renal artery stenosis is secondary so renin is high. Check the ratio.

## Q7

Neonate. Bilious vomiting day 1. Double bubble sign. Diagnosis?

- A. Pyloric stenosis
- B ✅. Duodenal atresia
- C. Malrotation with volvulus
- D. Necrotizing enterocolitis

**Answer: B.** Duodenal atresia

**Pivot:** Bilious vomiting day 1 plus double bubble equals duodenal atresia.

**Explanation:** Pyloric stenosis is non-bilious and presents at 3-6 weeks. Duodenal atresia is associated with Down syndrome.

## Q8

Microcytic anemia. Low serum iron, low TIBC, high ferritin. Diagnosis?

- A. Iron deficiency
- B ✅. Anemia of chronic disease
- C. Thalassemia
- D. Sideroblastic

**Answer: B.** Anemia of chronic disease

**Pivot:** TIBC direction is the pivot.

**Explanation:** Iron deficiency: low iron, HIGH TIBC, low ferritin. Chronic disease: low iron, LOW TIBC, high ferritin. Same iron, opposite TIBC.

## Q9

Fever, stiff neck. LP: low glucose, high protein, neutrophils. Diagnosis?

- A. Viral meningitis
- B ✅. Bacterial meningitis
- C. Fungal meningitis
- D. TB meningitis

**Answer: B.** Bacterial meningitis

**Pivot:** Glucose plus cell type splits meningitides.

**Explanation:** Bacterial: low glucose, neutrophils. Viral: normal glucose, lymphocytes. Fungal and TB: low glucose, lymphocytes.

## Q10

Hyperthyroidism with diffuse goiter, exophthalmos, increased RAIU throughout. Diagnosis?

- A. Toxic adenoma
- B ✅. Graves
- C. Subacute thyroiditis
- D. Hashimoto

**Answer: B.** Graves

**Pivot:** RAIU pattern splits hyperthyroid causes.

**Explanation:** Graves: diffuse increased uptake. Toxic adenoma: focal hot spot. Thyroiditis: decreased uptake. Exophthalmos is unique to Graves.

## Q11

- D. Minimal change

## Q12

Anti-dsDNA positive and low complement. Diagnosis?

- A. RA
- B. Sjögren
- C ✅. SLE
- D. Scleroderma

**Answer: C.** SLE

**Pivot:** Anti-dsDNA and anti-Smith are SLE-specific.

**Explanation:** ANA is sensitive but not specific. Anti-dsDNA and anti-Smith are highly specific. Anti-dsDNA correlates with lupus nephritis activity.

## Q13

Patient on long-term lithium. Polyuria. Urine osm stays low after desmopressin. Diagnosis?

- A. Central DI
- B ✅. Nephrogenic DI
- C. Primary polydipsia
- D. Osmotic diuresis

**Answer: B.** Nephrogenic DI

**Pivot:** Response to desmopressin splits central from nephrogenic DI.

**Explanation:** Central DI responds to desmopressin. Nephrogenic does not. Lithium is a classic cause of nephrogenic DI.

## Q14

Episodic palpitations and headache. Plasma metanephrines elevated. Diagnosis?

- A. Carcinoid
- B ✅. Pheochromocytoma
- C. Hyperthyroidism
- D. Panic disorder

**Answer: B.** Pheochromocytoma

**Pivot:** Plasma or urine metanephrines screen for pheo.

**Explanation:** Pheo gives episodic HTN, headache, palpitations, diaphoresis. Metanephrines are more reliable than catecholamines.

- D. CLL

## Q16

- A. Acetaminophen alone
- B. Oral antibiotics
- C ✅. IVIG plus high-dose aspirin
- D. Antivirals

**Answer: C.** IVIG plus high-dose aspirin

## Q17

60-year-old diabetic. Painless monocular vision loss. Cherry-red spot at macula. Diagnosis?

- A. CRVO
- B ✅. CRAO
- C. Vitreous hemorrhage
- D. Retinal detachment

**Answer: B.** CRAO

**Pivot:** Cherry-red spot at macula equals CRAO. Blood and thunder equals CRVO.

**Explanation:** CRAO: pale retina from ischemia, fovea preserved due to underlying choroid supply. CRVO: diffuse hemorrhages.

## Q18

Acute joint pain. Aspiration shows negatively birefringent needle-shaped crystals. Diagnosis?

- A ✅. Gout
- B. Pseudogout
- C. Septic arthritis
- D. RA

**Answer: A.** Gout

**Pivot:** Crystal shape and birefringence split gout from pseudogout.

**Explanation:** Gout: needle, negative birefringence, urate. Pseudogout: rhomboid, positive, CPPD. Memorize the pairs together.

## Q19

Neonate day 3. Bilious vomiting, bloody stools. Pneumatosis intestinalis on X-ray. Diagnosis?

- A. Hirschsprung
- B ✅. Necrotizing enterocolitis
- C. Meconium ileus
- D. Intussusception

**Answer: B.** Necrotizing enterocolitis

**Pivot:** Pneumatosis intestinalis equals NEC.

**Explanation:** Premature, formula-fed infants at highest risk. Gas in the bowel wall is pathognomonic.

## Q20

Progressive descending weakness, recent canned food, dilated unreactive pupils. Diagnosis?

- A. Guillain-Barré
- B ✅. Botulism
- C. Myasthenia gravis
- D. Lambert-Eaton

**Answer: B.** Botulism

**Pivot:** Direction of weakness and pupils split botulism from GBS.

**Explanation:** GBS: ascending, pupils spared. Botulism: descending, pupils dilated. Food history (home-canned, honey) is the historical clue.
