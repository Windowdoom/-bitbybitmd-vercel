SIGMA vs PI BOND

SIGMA: head-on overlap, electron density ON the internuclear axis, cylindrically symmetric, allows FREE rotation, present in every bond (single, and the first bond of double/triple). PI: sideways overlap of parallel p orbitals, density ABOVE and BELOW the axis, RESTRICTS rotation (rotating breaks it), and is the second/third bond of multiple bonds. Pi electrons are exposed and reactive; sigma electrons are buried and inert. Rotation locked = a pi bond is present.…
